HR Analytics: Strategic Attrition & Workforce Intelligence
📌 Project Overview
This project presents a comprehensive HR Analytics Dashboard designed to track, visualize, and analyze workforce dynamics. By leveraging a dataset of 1,470 employees, the dashboard identifies the primary drivers of an overall 16.1% attrition rate. It serves as a decision-support tool for HR departments to transition from reactive reporting to proactive, data-driven retention strategies.

🛠️ Tech Stack
Business Intelligence Tool: Microsoft Power BI

Data Transformation: Power Query (Advanced ETL, data profiling, and cleaning)

Data Modeling: DAX (Data Analysis Expressions) for custom measures and KPIs

UI/UX: Modern Dark-Theme interface designed for high-impact executive reporting

📂 Data Attributes
The analysis is based on a dataset containing 1,470 employee records across several dimensions:

Demographics: Detailed segments for Age, Gender, and Education (Medical, Life Sciences, Marketing, etc.).

Employment Details: Departmental mapping (R&D, Sales, HR) and Job Roles.

Retention Metrics: Tenure (Years at Company), Work Experience, and Salary Slabs.

✨ Features & Highlights
📉 Business Problem
High employee turnover impacts organizational stability and increases recruitment costs. The objective of this project was to diagnose why 237 employees left the organization and to identify if attrition was localized within specific job roles, salary brackets, or tenure milestones.

🎯 Dashboard Goal
To provide a centralized, interactive environment where stakeholders can drill down into departmental performance. The dashboard enables users to isolate variables and identify high-risk employee segments through dynamic filtering and cross-visual interaction.

🔍 Visual Walkthrough & Insights
Executive KPIs: Real-time visibility into the Total Employee Count (1,470), Attrition Count (237), and Average Age (37).

Job Role Analysis: A horizontal bar chart reveals that Laboratory Technicians and Sales Executives experience the highest attrition volume, indicating potential role-specific friction points.

Compensation Correlation: The "Attrition by Salary Slab" visual demonstrates that the majority of exits occur in the lowest salary bracket (Upto 5K), suggesting a direct link between compensation levels and retention.

Tenure Trends: An area chart highlights a critical "danger zone" within the first 2 years of service, signaling a need for improved early-stage engagement.

Demographic Distribution: A donut chart tracks the educational background of departing staff, showing 38% belong to the Life Sciences sector.

💡 Business Impact
Data-Driven Retention: Pinpointed high-risk segments (Age 18–35 in entry-level roles), allowing for targeted intervention.

Strategic Resource Allocation: Identified the need for competitive benchmarking for technical roles like Lab Technicians.

Process Optimization: Highlighted the importance of the first 24 months of the employee lifecycle in reducing long-term turnover.
